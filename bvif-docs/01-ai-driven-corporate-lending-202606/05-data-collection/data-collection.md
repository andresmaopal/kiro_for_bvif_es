## Metric 1: Campaign Conversion Rate*
- **Category**: Grow Revenue
- **Definition**: (Corporate prospects that became loan clients ÷ Total prospects contacted) × 100
- **Current Baseline (Q1 2026)**: est. 4.5%
- **Historical Trend**:
  | Quarter | Value |
  |---|---|
  | Q1 2024 | est. 3.8% |
  | Q2 2024 | est. 4.0% |
  | Q3 2024 | est. 3.9% |
  | Q4 2024 | est. 4.2% |
  | Q1 2025 | est. 4.1% |
  | Q2 2025 | est. 4.3% |
  | Q3 2025 | est. 4.4% |
  | Q4 2025 | est. 4.5% |
- **Financial Impact per Unit**: Each 1 percentage point increase in conversion rate ≈ 15 additional corporate loans per year (based on ~1,500 prospects contacted annually). At an average corporate loan size of USD 500K and net interest margin of ~4%, each additional loan ≈ USD 20K annual interest income. So 1 p.p. improvement ≈ USD 300K/year.
- **Industry Benchmark**: Best-in-class banks with AI-driven targeting achieve 8–12% conversion on corporate campaigns ([BCG 2025 corporate lending report](https://www.bcg.com/publications/2025/strategies-smarter-corporate-lending)).
- **Improvement Target**: 7.5% (≈ 3 p.p. improvement, or ~67% increase)
- **Target Rationale**: AI-driven prospect scoring can significantly improve targeting accuracy. A move from 4.5% to 7.5% is conservative relative to best-in-class (8–12%) and accounts for BGR's market position and data maturity.
- **Data provenance**: All values are estimates based on industry benchmarks and user-provided context.

- **Financial Impact per Unit**: Each additional corporate loan ≈ USD 500K average size × 4% net interest margin = USD 20K annual interest income per loan.
- **Industry Benchmark**: A mid-size bank with AI-enhanced origination typically grows corporate loan volumes 20–35% in the first year post-implementation ([McKinsey digital lending insights](https://www.mckinsey.com/business-functions/risk-and-resilience/our-insights/the-lending-revolution-how-digital-credit-is-changing-banks-from-the-inside)).
- **Improvement Target**: 60 loans/quarter (+33%, or +15 loans/quarter)
- **Target Rationale**: Combines two effects — better targeting brings more qualified prospects, and faster decisions close more of them. A 33% increase is aligned with McKinsey's 20–35% range for AI-enhanced lending.
- **Data provenance**: All values are estimates based on industry benchmarks and user-provided context (USD 3,000 MM bank size).

## Metric 3: Average Time-to-Decision
- **Category**: Grow Revenue
- **Definition**: Average business days from complete application to final credit decision
- **Current Baseline (Q1 2026)**: 15 business days (3 weeks — confirmed by user)
- **Historical Trend**:
  | Quarter | Value |
  |---|---|
  | Q1 2024 | est. 18 days |
  | Q2 2024 | est. 17 days |
  | Q3 2024 | est. 17 days |
  | Q4 2024 | est. 16 days |
  | Q1 2025 | est. 16 days |
  | Q2 2025 | est. 15 days |
  | Q3 2025 | est. 15 days |
  | Q4 2025 | est. 15 days |
- **Financial Impact per Unit**: Each day reduced in time-to-decision reduces client attrition to competitors. Industry estimates suggest a 50% reduction in cycle time can improve win rates by 10–15% on competitive deals. At 45 loans/quarter with ~30% being competitive situations, a 50% time reduction → ~2 additional wins/quarter → USD 40K/year additional income.
- **Industry Benchmark**: AI-assisted corporate lending achieves 5–7 business days for standard corporate credits ([McKinsey](https://www.mckinsey.com/business-functions/risk-and-resilience/our-insights/the-lending-revolution-how-digital-credit-is-changing-banks-from-the-inside); [Backbase 2025](https://www.backbase.com/blog/ai-loan-origination)).
- **Improvement Target**: 7 business days (53% reduction)
- **Target Rationale**: Moving from 15 to 7 days aligns with the industry benchmark for AI-assisted processes. The remaining 7 days account for human review, compliance checks, and committee approvals that cannot be fully automated.
- **Data provenance**: Baseline confirmed by user (~3 weeks). Historical trend and targets are estimates based on industry benchmarks.

## Metric 4: Corporate Lending Revenue (Interest Income)
- **Category**: Grow Revenue
- **Definition**: Total interest income from corporate loan portfolio per quarter (USD)
- **Current Baseline (Q1 2026)**: est. USD 7.5M/quarter
- **Historical Trend**:
  | Quarter | Value |
  |---|---|
  | Q1 2024 | est. USD 6.2M |
  | Q2 2024 | est. USD 6.4M |
  | Q3 2024 | est. USD 6.5M |
  | Q4 2024 | est. USD 6.8M |
  | Q1 2025 | est. USD 7.0M |
  | Q2 2025 | est. USD 7.1M |
  | Q3 2025 | est. USD 7.3M |
  | Q4 2025 | est. USD 7.5M |
- **Financial Impact per Unit**: Direct revenue — every dollar of incremental interest income flows to the topline. Contribution margin for corporate lending ≈ 60–70% after cost of funds and operating costs.
- **Industry Benchmark**: For a USD 3,000 MM bank, corporate lending typically represents 25–35% of total assets. At 4% net interest margin on a ~USD 750M corporate portfolio = ~USD 30M annual income (USD 7.5M/quarter).
- **Improvement Target**: USD 8.7M/quarter (+16%, or +USD 1.2M/quarter)
- **Target Rationale**: The 16% revenue increase comes from more loans originated (33% more loans × same average size × NIM). Not all loans close in Q1, so annualized effect is ~USD 4.8M additional interest income per year.
- **Data provenance**: All values are estimates derived from bank size (USD 3,000 MM), typical corporate portfolio share (~25%), and standard NIM (~4%).

## Metric 5: Cost per Credit Decision*
- **Category**: Increase Operational Efficiency
- **Definition**: Total origination cost (analyst salaries + overhead) ÷ Number of corporate credit decisions per quarter
- **Current Baseline (Q1 2026)**: est. USD 2,800 per decision
- **Historical Trend**:
  | Quarter | Value |
  |---|---|
  | Q1 2024 | est. USD 2,950 |
  | Q2 2024 | est. USD 2,900 |
  | Q3 2024 | est. USD 2,900 |
  | Q4 2024 | est. USD 2,850 |
  | Q1 2025 | est. USD 2,850 |
  | Q2 2025 | est. USD 2,800 |
  | Q3 2025 | est. USD 2,800 |
  | Q4 2025 | est. USD 2,800 |
- **Financial Impact per Unit**: Each USD 100 reduction in cost per decision × 180 decisions/year = USD 18,000/year savings.
- **Industry Benchmark**: AI-optimized commercial lending operations achieve USD 1,500–2,000 per corporate credit decision ([Deloitte 2024: 30–40% cost reduction](https://www.deloitte.com/lu/en/our-thinking/future-of-advice/how-to-streamline-the-credit-journey.html)).
- **Improvement Target**: USD 1,900 per decision (32% reduction)
- **Target Rationale**: A 32% reduction aligns with Deloitte's 30–40% benchmark for AI-streamlined credit processes. Savings come from automating document processing, preliminary analysis, and initial scoring.
- **Data provenance**: Estimates based on team of ~12 analysts at ~USD 35K avg. annual cost in Ecuador + overhead, producing ~45 decisions/quarter.

- **Historical Trend**:
  | Quarter | Value |
  |---|---|
  | Q1 2024 | est. 3.2 |
  | Q2 2024 | est. 3.3 |
  | Q3 2024 | est. 3.3 |
  | Q4 2024 | est. 3.5 |
  | Q1 2025 | est. 3.4 |
  | Q2 2025 | est. 3.6 |
  | Q3 2025 | est. 3.7 |
  | Q4 2025 | est. 3.75 |
- **Financial Impact per Unit**: Each additional decision per analyst per quarter means the bank handles more volume without hiring. At USD 2,800 per decision cost and maintaining headcount flat, productivity gains directly translate to serving more clients with existing resources.
- **Industry Benchmark**: AI-assisted analysts in corporate lending typically handle 5–7 decisions/quarter (40–80% improvement over manual processes) ([BCG 2025](https://www.bcg.com/publications/2025/strategies-smarter-corporate-lending)).
- **Improvement Target**: 5.0 decisions/analyst/quarter (+33%)
- **Target Rationale**: Aligns with 33% more loan volume handled by the same 12-analyst team. Conservative relative to BCG's 40–80% improvement range.
- **Data provenance**: Estimates based on 12 analysts and 45 decisions/quarter (derived from bank size and typical corporate lending volumes).

## Metric 7: Manual Processing Hours per Application
- **Category**: Increase Operational Efficiency
- **Definition**: Total analyst hours on document gathering + financial analysis + credit memo ÷ Applications processed
- **Current Baseline (Q1 2026)**: est. 40 hours per application
- **Historical Trend**:
  | Quarter | Value |
  |---|---|
  | Q1 2024 | est. 45 hours |
  | Q2 2024 | est. 44 hours |
  | Q3 2024 | est. 43 hours |
  | Q4 2024 | est. 42 hours |
  | Q1 2025 | est. 42 hours |
  | Q2 2025 | est. 41 hours |
  | Q3 2025 | est. 40 hours |
  | Q4 2025 | est. 40 hours |
- **Financial Impact per Unit**: Each hour saved × analyst cost (~USD 18/hour in Ecuador, fully loaded) = USD 18 savings per hour per application. At 180 applications/year, each 1-hour reduction = USD 3,240/year.
- **Industry Benchmark**: AI-assisted origination typically reduces manual processing time by 40–60%, bringing corporate credit analysis to 16–24 hours per application ([Deloitte 2024](https://www.deloitte.com/lu/en/our-thinking/future-of-advice/how-to-streamline-the-credit-journey.html)).
- **Improvement Target**: 22 hours per application (45% reduction)
- **Target Rationale**: A 45% reduction is mid-range of the 40–60% benchmark. AI automates document extraction, preliminary financial analysis, and initial risk scoring — the most time-consuming manual steps.
- **Data provenance**: Estimates based on 3-week cycle (~15 business days × ~2.7 productive hours/day dedicated to each credit = ~40 hours), consistent with user's statement about cycle duration.
