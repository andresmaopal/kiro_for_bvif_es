# Banco General Rumiñahui — AI-Driven Corporate Lending: Business Value Summary

## Executive Summary

Banco General Rumiñahui (BGR), a mid-size retail bank with USD 3,000 million in assets, proposes implementing an AI-driven solution to transform its corporate credit origination process. Today, this process is largely manual — analysts spend approximately 3 weeks per corporate credit decision, the bank struggles with ineffective prospect targeting for corporate lending campaigns, and risk assessment quality varies across analysts.

Using the AI Business Value Identification Framework (AI-BVIF), we evaluated this initiative across two primary business value categories: **Grow Revenue** (better targeting and faster decisions → more corporate loans) and **Increase Operational Efficiency** (lower cost per credit decision through automation). We identified 7 metrics, assessed feasibility (all measurable with existing or easily derivable data), collected industry benchmarks, and calculated the expected annualized business value.

**The AI-Driven Corporate Lending initiative is estimated to deliver USD ~4.96 million in total annualized business value** — comprising USD 4.8M in additional interest income from revenue growth and USD 162K in direct cost savings from operational efficiency. On a bottom-line equivalent basis (adjusting revenue for contribution margin), the initiative delivers approximately **USD 3.28 million per year**.

## Strategic Value Beyond the Numbers

Beyond the quantified USD 4.96M annual impact, this initiative delivers strategic benefits that are difficult to monetize but materially important:

- **Competitive positioning**: BGR moves from a 3-week decision cycle to under 2 weeks — closing the gap with larger banks and fintechs that have already digitized their corporate lending. In Ecuador's banking market, this is a differentiator.
- **Data foundation for future AI**: The data infrastructure built for AI credit origination (structured financial data, decision outcomes, risk model feedback loops) becomes the foundation for future initiatives — cross-sell models, portfolio risk monitoring, early warning systems.
- **Workforce transformation**: Credit analysts evolve from document processors to strategic advisors. This improves retention, attracts better talent, and positions the team for higher-value work as the bank grows.
- **Scalability without linear cost growth**: The AI-augmented model allows BGR to grow its corporate lending book 30–50% without proportional headcount increases — a structural advantage as the bank pursues growth.
- **Risk consistency**: While not a primary value category, AI-driven standardized assessment reduces analyst-to-analyst variability in credit decisions, improving overall portfolio quality over time.

## Risk of Inaction

If BGR does **not** pursue AI-driven corporate lending:

- **Revenue stagnation**: At the current 4.5% campaign conversion rate and 3-week cycle, the corporate lending book grows at organic pace (~5% annually) while competitors with AI capabilities capture the best corporate clients faster.
- **Competitive erosion**: Larger Ecuadorian banks and regional players are investing in digital lending. BGR risks being perceived as slow and manual, losing client relationships to faster-moving institutions.
- **Rising cost per decision**: As regulatory requirements grow and corporate credit complexity increases, the manual process will require more analyst hours — pushing cost per decision above USD 3,000+ without intervention.
- **Talent risk**: Experienced analysts assigned to repetitive document work will increasingly seek roles at institutions offering more strategic, technology-augmented positions.
- **Missed data opportunity**: Every year without structured AI infrastructure is a year of unstructured decision data that cannot feed future models. The compounding value of data captured today grows exponentially.

## Appendix A: Initiative Definition Summary

**Banco General Rumiñahui (BGR)** is a mid-size retail bank (USD 3,000 MM assets) serving both retail and corporate customers in Ecuador. The bank proposes implementing AI to transform its corporate credit origination — from prospect identification through credit decision.

**Current state**: Largely manual. Analysts gather documents, manually review financials, produce credit memos. The process takes ~3 weeks, is inconsistent across analysts, costs ~USD 2,800 per decision, and targets the wrong corporate prospects for lending campaigns.

**Future state with AI**: Automated document processing, AI-driven prospect scoring, standardized preliminary risk assessment. Target: 7-day decisions, 7.5% campaign conversion, USD 1,900 cost per decision, 33% more volume with the same team.

**Sponsor**: Gerente de Crédito (Credit VP). Aligned stakeholders: CEO/President (measured on growth), CIO (measured on technology business impact).

## Appendix B: Methodology

This business value assessment followed the **AI Business Value Identification Framework (AI-BVIF)** — a structured, seven-stage process organized in three phases:

1. **Discovery Phase** (Stages 1–2): Refined the initiative definition and mapped it to CAF v3 business value categories (Grow Revenue + Increase Operational Efficiency).
2. **Metrics Phase** (Stages 3–4): Identified 7 metrics across both categories, assessed feasibility (5×F1, 2×F2, 0×F3), and confirmed all proceed without proxies or elimination.
3. **Quantification Phase** (Stages 5–7): Collected data using industry benchmarks and user-provided context (estimate-based approach), calculated annualized financial values, checked for double-counting, and produced this final document.

**Data approach**: The user elected to use industry benchmarks and reasonable estimates (from McKinsey, BCG, Deloitte, and Backbase research) rather than providing internal actuals. All values are clearly labeled as estimates and should be validated with BGR's internal data before presenting to decision-makers.

**Important**: AI-BVIF quantifies the **value side only**. Cost of implementation, operational cost, risk, and timing must be evaluated separately before this becomes a full business case. This document provides a grounded, auditable estimate of potential business value to support informed decision-making — it is not a substitute for that broader analysis.
