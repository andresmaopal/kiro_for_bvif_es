# Business Value Mapping

## Primary Category: Grow Revenue

Our AI-driven corporate lending initiative directly contributes to topline growth in two ways. First, by using AI to identify the right corporate prospects for our lending campaigns, we will significantly improve our campaign conversion rates — reaching companies that actually match our risk appetite and credit products instead of wasting outreach on poor-fit targets. This means more corporate clients closing, more loans originated, and more interest income generated. Second, by dramatically reducing our time-to-decision, we stop losing corporate clients to faster-moving competitors. In a market where days matter, the bank that says "yes" first often wins the relationship. Both effects expand our addressable corporate lending portfolio and drive measurable revenue growth — which is exactly what our CEO and President are measured on.

## Secondary Category: Increase Operational Efficiency

The initiative will reduce the cost per corporate credit decision by automating the most time-consuming parts of the origination process — document gathering, preliminary financial analysis, and initial risk scoring. Today, each corporate credit application requires significant analyst hours. With AI handling the structured, repetitive work, our analysts can focus on high-value judgment calls for complex cases. The same team will process more applications without proportional headcount growth. This directly reduces our cost-to-originate ratio and improves the productivity of our credit function — a bottom-line improvement that compounds as volume grows.

---

**Why not "Reduce Business Risk"?**

While more consistent AI-driven risk assessment is a real benefit, it is a byproduct of the operational efficiency improvement rather than the initiative's primary purpose. The initiative was not conceived to address a compliance gap or security concern — it was conceived to grow the lending book faster and more efficiently. We acknowledge the risk-quality improvement but do not elevate it to a primary or secondary category because its impact is indirect and subordinate to the revenue and efficiency drivers.
