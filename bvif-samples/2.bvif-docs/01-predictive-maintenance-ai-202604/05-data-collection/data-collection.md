# Data Collection

## Metric 1: Unplanned Downtime Rate %
- **Category**: Increase Operational Efficiency
- **Definition**: (Unplanned Downtime Hours / Total Available Hours) × 100
- **Current Baseline (Q4 2025)**: 2.8% (324 hours of unplanned downtime out of 11,520 total available hours)
- **Historical Trend**:
  | Quarter | Value |
  |---|---|
  | Q2 2024 | 2.7% (312 hours) |
  | Q3 2024 | 2.5% (287 hours) |
  | Q4 2024 | 2.6% (305 hours) |
  | Q1 2025 | 2.8% (318 hours) |
  | Q2 2025 | 3.0% (342 hours) |
  | Q3 2025 | 2.9% (336 hours) |
  | Q4 2025 | 2.8% (324 hours) |
  | Q1 2026 | 2.8% (current) |
- **Financial Impact per Unit**: Each hour of unplanned downtime results in approximately $11,500 in lost production value. More specifically: 86.7 units lost per hour × $250 average revenue per unit = $21,675 revenue impact per hour, with contribution margin impact of $9,971 per hour (46% margin).
- **Industry Benchmark**: Leading furniture manufacturers maintain unplanned downtime rates below 1.5% (Woodworking Machinery Industry Association)
- **Improvement Target**: 1.0-1.4% (50-65% reduction)
- **Target Rationale**: Industry benchmarks and pilot data from similar manufacturers implementing predictive maintenance show unplanned downtime reduction of 50-65% is achievable within 12-18 months. Target of 1.0-1.4% approaches industry best practice of 1.5%.

## Metric 2: Mean Time Between Failures (MTBF)
- **Category**: Increase Operational Efficiency
- **Definition**: Total Operating Hours / Number of Unplanned Failures
- **Current Baseline (Q4 2025)**: 4,114 hours (42 unplanned equipment failures across 450 machines with total equipment operating time of 172,800 hours)
- **Historical Trend**:
  | Quarter | Value |
  |---|---|
  | Q2 2024 | 4,425 hours (39 failures) |
  | Q3 2024 | 4,520 hours (38 failures) |
  | Q4 2024 | 4,365 hours (40 failures) |
  | Q1 2025 | 4,245 hours (41 failures) |
  | Q2 2025 | 3,885 hours (45 failures) |
  | Q3 2025 | 4,020 hours (43 failures) |
  | Q4 2025 | 4,114 hours (42 failures) |
  | Q1 2026 | 4,114 hours (current) |
- **Financial Impact per Unit**: Higher MTBF means fewer unplanned failures, which directly reduces unplanned downtime. Each failure results in an average of 7.7 hours of downtime per emergency event. With fewer failures, there is less production loss ($9,971 per hour in lost contribution margin), lower emergency repair costs (overtime labor at 1.5-2× standard rates, expedited parts with 40-60% premium), and reduced external contractor callouts.
- **Industry Benchmark**: Best-in-class furniture manufacturers achieve MTBF rates exceeding 6,000 hours through condition-based maintenance programs
- **Improvement Target**: 5,554-5,965 hours (35-45% improvement)
- **Target Rationale**: MTBF improvement of 35-45% is realistic as condition-based maintenance prevents premature failures. Target of 5,554-5,965 hours approaches best-in-class 6,000+ hours.

## Metric 3: Maintenance Cost per Unit Produced
- **Category**: Increase Operational Efficiency
- **Definition**: Total Maintenance Costs (Planned + Unplanned) / Total Units Produced
- **Current Baseline (2025 Annual)**: $12.00 per unit ($8,640,000 total maintenance costs ÷ 720,000 units produced)
- **Historical Trend**:
  | Period | Value |
  |---|---|
  | 2023 | $10.85/unit ($7,470,000 total / 688,000 units) |
  | 2024 | $11.42/unit ($7,980,000 total / 698,500 units) |
  | 2025 | $12.00/unit ($8,640,000 total / 720,000 units) |
- **Cost Breakdown (2025)**:
  - Scheduled preventive maintenance labor: $3,240,000 (37.5%)
  - Emergency reactive repair labor: $2,880,000 (33.3%)
  - Replacement parts and components: $1,920,000 (22.2%)
  - External contractor services: $600,000 (7.0%)
- **Financial Impact per Unit**: This metric directly represents cost per unit. Annual production volume is 720,000 units. Maintenance costs per unit have increased 10.6% over two years, driven primarily by emergency reactive repair costs which grew from $2,160,000 in 2023 to $2,880,000 in 2025 (33% increase).
- **Industry Benchmark**: Comparable furniture manufacturers report maintenance costs between $9.50 and $10.50 per unit (PrecisionFurniture is operating 14-26% above industry efficiency levels)
- **Improvement Target**: $8.40-$9.60 per unit (20-30% reduction)
- **Target Rationale**: Peer case studies document maintenance cost reductions of 20-30% with predictive maintenance. Target of $8.40-$9.60 approaches industry benchmark of $9.50-$10.50.

## Metric 4: Unplanned Work Order Rate
- **Category**: Increase Operational Efficiency
- **Definition**: Number of Unplanned Work Orders / Total Work Orders × 100
- **Current Baseline (Q4 2025)**: 15.0% (252 reactive maintenance events out of 1,680 total maintenance interventions)
- **Historical Trend**:
  | Quarter | Value |
  |---|---|
  | Q2 2024 | 14.5% (244 reactive / 1,680 total) |
  | Q3 2024 | 14.2% (238 reactive / 1,680 total) |
  | Q4 2024 | 14.9% (250 reactive / 1,680 total) |
  | Q1 2025 | 15.4% (259 reactive / 1,680 total) |
  | Q2 2025 | 16.8% (282 reactive / 1,680 total) |
  | Q3 2025 | 16.1% (270 reactive / 1,680 total) |
  | Q4 2025 | 15.0% (252 reactive / 1,680 total) |
  | Q1 2026 | 15.0% (current) |
- **Financial Impact per Unit**: Each reactive event is significantly more expensive than scheduled maintenance due to: unplanned production line stoppages (average 7.7 hours per emergency event), overtime labor rates (1.5-2× standard rates), expedited parts shipping (40-60% premium to parts costs), and external contractor callouts. Operations leadership estimates emergency events cost 3-4× more per intervention than scheduled preventive maintenance.
- **Industry Benchmark**: Organizations with mature predictive maintenance programs achieve reactive maintenance rates below 8%, with 75-85% of maintenance events being condition-based and proactive
- **Improvement Target**: 8.0% or below (approximately 47% reduction in reactive maintenance events)
- **Target Rationale**: Emergency work order rates below 8% are standard for mature predictive maintenance programs, representing a shift from reactive to proactive maintenance.

## Metric 5: Planned vs Unplanned Maintenance Ratio
- **Category**: Increase Operational Efficiency
- **Definition**: Planned Maintenance Hours / Unplanned Maintenance Hours
- **Current Baseline (Q4 2025)**: 5.67:1 (1,428 planned maintenance events / 252 unplanned events = 5.67, or 85% planned vs 15% unplanned)
- **Historical Trend**:
  | Quarter | Value |
  |---|---|
  | Q2 2024 | 5.89:1 (1,436 planned / 244 unplanned) |
  | Q3 2024 | 6.05:1 (1,442 planned / 238 unplanned) |
  | Q4 2024 | 5.72:1 (1,430 planned / 250 unplanned) |
  | Q1 2025 | 5.49:1 (1,421 planned / 259 unplanned) |
  | Q2 2025 | 4.96:1 (1,398 planned / 282 unplanned) |
  | Q3 2025 | 5.22:1 (1,410 planned / 270 unplanned) |
  | Q4 2025 | 5.67:1 (1,428 planned / 252 unplanned) |
  | Q1 2026 | 5.67:1 (current) |
- **Financial Impact per Unit**: Planned maintenance is significantly more cost-effective than reactive maintenance. Benefits include: scheduled maintenance during planned downtime windows (no production disruption), standard labor rates instead of overtime (1.5-2× premium), normal parts ordering instead of expedited shipping (40-60% premium), better resource planning and utilization, and elimination of external contractor callouts.
- **Industry Benchmark**: Organizations with mature predictive maintenance programs achieve reactive maintenance rates below 8%, meaning the ratio would be approximately 11.5:1 or higher (92% planned / 8% unplanned)
- **Improvement Target**: 11.5:1 (92% planned / 8% unplanned)
- **Target Rationale**: Target aligns with industry best practices for mature predictive maintenance programs, representing a fundamental shift from reactive to proactive maintenance approach.

## Metric 6: On-Time Delivery Rate %
- **Category**: Grow Revenue
- **Definition**: (Orders Delivered On-Time / Total Orders) × 100
- **Current Baseline (Q4 2025)**: 90.0% (2,106 orders delivered on-time out of 2,340 total orders)
- **Historical Trend**:
  | Quarter | Value |
  |---|---|
  | Q1 2024 | 92.4% (2,186 on-time / 2,365 total) |
  | Q2 2024 | 91.8% (2,175 on-time / 2,370 total) |
  | Q3 2024 | 91.2% (2,159 on-time / 2,368 total) |
  | Q4 2024 | 90.6% (2,138 on-time / 2,360 total) |
  | Q1 2025 | 91.1% (2,142 on-time / 2,352 total) |
  | Q2 2025 | 88.5% (2,084 on-time / 2,355 total) |
  | Q3 2025 | 89.2% (2,095 on-time / 2,349 total) |
  | Q4 2025 | 90.0% (2,106 on-time / 2,340 total) |
- **Financial Impact per Unit**: Late deliveries result in: contractual penalties (averaging $2,500 per late order for large accounts, estimated $400K-$600K annually), damaged customer relationships and trust erosion, risk of contract non-renewal during annual negotiations, and lost competitive differentiation. The CRO estimates the company loses approximately $2.5M in potential contract value annually due to delivery-related customer concerns. Root cause analysis shows 68% of late deliveries in 2025 were directly attributable to unplanned production downtime.
- **Industry Benchmark**: Leading B2B furniture manufacturers consistently maintain OTD rates above 95%, with best-in-class organizations achieving 97-98%
- **Improvement Target**: 95.0% (5 percentage point improvement)
- **Target Rationale**: OTD rate improvement to 94-96% is feasible by eliminating downtime-related delivery delays. Target of 95.0% reaches industry benchmark and addresses the primary cause of late deliveries (unplanned downtime).

## Metric 7: Production Capacity Utilization %
- **Category**: Grow Revenue
- **Definition**: (Actual Production Output / Maximum Production Capacity) × 100
- **Current Baseline (Q4 2025)**: 84.4% (28,080 units lost due to unplanned downtime out of 180,000 expected quarterly production = 15.6% loss rate, meaning 84.4% utilization)
- **Historical Trend**:
  | Quarter | Value |
  |---|---|
  | Q1 2024 | 85.6% (25,092 units lost / 174,625 expected) |
  | Q2 2024 | 85.0% (26,198 units lost / 174,625 expected) |
  | Q3 2024 | 86.2% (24,098 units lost / 174,625 expected) |
  | Q4 2024 | 85.3% (25,670 units lost / 174,625 expected) |
  | Q1 2025 | 84.7% (27,540 units lost / 180,000 expected) |
  | Q2 2025 | 83.5% (29,700 units lost / 180,000 expected) |
  | Q3 2025 | 83.8% (29,160 units lost / 180,000 expected) |
  | Q4 2025 | 84.4% (28,080 units lost / 180,000 expected) |
- **Financial Impact per Unit**: Each hour of unplanned downtime results in 86.7 units lost. At average unit revenue of $250 and contribution margin of 46%, each hour represents: Revenue impact of $21,675 per hour ($250 × 86.7 units), Contribution margin impact of $9,971 per hour ($21,675 × 46%). In Q4 2025, 28,080 units lost × $250 = $7.02M revenue exposure, with $3.23M in lost contribution margin.
- **Industry Benchmark**: Best-in-class furniture manufacturers with mature predictive maintenance programs typically achieve 92-95% capacity utilization, with unplanned downtime rates below 1.5% (estimated)
- **Improvement Target**: 92.2-94.9% (50-65% downtime reduction)
- **Target Rationale**: With 50-65% reduction in unplanned downtime, capacity utilization can improve from 84.4% to 92.2% (50% downtime reduction) or 94.9% (65% downtime reduction), approaching industry best practice of 92-95%.

## Additional Context

### Annual Production Volume
720,000 units per year (2025 actual production)

### Average Revenue per Unit
$250 per unit average (Company generates approximately $180M in annual revenue with 720,000 units)

### Contribution Margin
46% contribution margin (revenue minus variable costs)

### Key Operational Context
- Three manufacturing facilities (Ohio, North Carolina, Texas)
- 450 pieces of critical production equipment
- 11 production lines operating two shifts each (16 hours/day, 5 days/week)
- 11,520 total available production hours per quarter across all sites
- Manufacturing process is 85% automated
