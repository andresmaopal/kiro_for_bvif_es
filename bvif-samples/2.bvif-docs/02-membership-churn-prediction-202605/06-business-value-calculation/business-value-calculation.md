# Business Value Calculation

## Headline numbers driving the math

- Members active Jan 1, 2025: **78,200**
- 1 percentage point of renewal = **782 members** retained
- Improvement target: **+10 pp** annual renewal rate (54% → 64%)
- Members retained vs. baseline: **7,820/year**
- Gross margin per retained member: **$470/year**
- Blended CAC per member: **$47**

## Double-Counting Analysis

### Inside the Grow Revenue category

- **Metrics 1, 2, 3** all measure the same underlying phenomenon — the cohort of sócio members who stay. Financial value is attributed **only to Metric 1**. Metrics 2 and 3 are kept on the dashboard as operational/financial reporting metrics.
- **Metric 4 (match-day spend)** *could* compound on top of Metric 1 — retained sócios continue to attend and spend on match day. However, attributing additional revenue here would risk optimistic double-counting (the marginal retained member's match-day spend depends heavily on which sócio segment they fall into). We hold this at $0 deliberately.

### Across categories

- **Metric 5 (Avoided CAC)** vs **Metric 1 (Renewal value)**: No overlap. Metric 1 captures the gross margin from a member that stays. Metric 5 captures the acquisition spend the club avoids by not having to replace that member. They are additive.

## Risk-of-inaction baseline

If the 2024→2025 trend continues unchecked (renewal rate dropped 3 pp YoY; total exits grew from 32,700 to 36,000, with both voluntary and lapsed components rising), the club faces:
- Continued net member loss in 2026 (extrapolated −4,500 to −5,500 members).
- Membership revenue declining for the second consecutive year.
- Compounding loss of high-attendance sócios who anchor match-day F&B and merchandise spend.
- Continued payment-failure lapses growing in absolute terms as the base ages — the most directly addressable component if the model goes live.

This baseline is not deducted from the quantified value above (Stage 6 measures *uplift vs. status quo*) but provides the strategic context for Stage 7.
