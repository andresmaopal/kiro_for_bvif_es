# Business Value Mapping

## Primary Category: Grow Revenue

This initiative directly protects and grows the sócio torcedor revenue line. The mechanism is straightforward: a retained member generates ~$541 in annual revenue; a churned member generates zero. By identifying at-risk members early and intervening before cancellation, the initiative:

- **Lifts the renewal rate** — moves from the current 54% toward Brazilian Série A best-in-class (66%) and approaches industry peers like English Premiership Rugby (62–74%).
- **Reverses the net-decline trend** — 2025 was the first year on record with a net loss of members. Sustained renewal rate improvement reverses this.
- **Compounds ancillary revenue** — long-tenured high-attendance sócios spend 48% more on match day than the blended fan average, so retention also protects food & beverage and merchandise revenue streams.

This is recurring topline revenue, not cost reduction, and it maps cleanly to **Grow Revenue**.

## Secondary Category: Increase Operational Efficiency

A smaller secondary effect: every member retained is a member the club does not need to acquire. With a blended cost-to-acquire of $47 per member, retaining 2,500–4,500 members per year that would otherwise have churned avoids $0.12M–$0.21M in acquisition spend.

This is a real benefit but materially smaller than the revenue effect, and it is captured here for completeness rather than as a primary driver.

## Categories Considered and Rejected

- **Improve Customer Experience**: Membership churn does correlate with experience, but the financial mechanism is captured by renewal rate (Grow Revenue). Adding CX as a separate category would risk double-counting against the renewal lift.
- **Drive Innovation / New Capabilities**: This initiative uses existing data and adopts proven techniques. It is not a net-new capability play.
